# Project Overview

## Context

This project was completed as an AUT academic-industry collaboration with Nestlé New Zealand. The team explored forecasting and reporting approaches that could support more forward-looking analysis and reduce manual review work.

The public case study focuses only on the reporting side of the project. Confidential business context, product-level information, internal figures, and client materials are excluded.

## Reporting objective

The reporting objective was to make forecasting-related outputs easier to review by connecting structured assumptions with a clear Power BI reporting view.

The work needed to support three practical questions:

1. What assumptions or selections were used?
2. What reporting output was produced from those inputs?
3. How should a non-technical user interpret the result and its limitations?

## Why the reporting layer mattered

A forecasting output is not automatically a useful reporting product. A stakeholder also needs:

- clear input assumptions
- a consistent view of the output
- suitable KPI labels and context
- documented limitations
- a handover path that another person can follow

This reporting layer was the main area where I contributed.

## Project status

The work was an academic prototype and handover exercise. It was not a production deployment, and this repository does not claim operational adoption or measured business impact.
